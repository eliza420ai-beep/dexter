## Core Investment Case

### 1. Revenue share plus buybacks create asymmetric upside

- Hyperliquid is the largest revenue-generating project in crypto outside stablecoins.
- About **97% of protocol revenue** is used to buy back and burn, or otherwise distribute value to, `HYPE` holders.
- There is no cleaner liquid token expression of exchange fee capture at this scale.

The result is simple: even in a choppy or mildly bearish crypto tape, `HYPE` can still compound in USD terms if the venue keeps printing fees and retiring supply.

### 2. It does not need a broad alt bull market

The setup does **not** require global crypto perp volume to explode.

- A gain of only about **3.97 percentage points** in global perp market share can move annualized revenue from roughly **$843 million** back to about **$1.4 billion**.
- That is realistic because Hyperliquid went from nonexistence to dominant perp DEX in roughly three years.
- The share-gain case is a market-structure story, not a reflexive speculative mania story.

In other words: `HYPE` can work even if the average altcoin keeps getting euthanized.

- Price discovery can happen on-chain **24/7**, including weekends when traditional venues are closed.

Base assumption:

- HIP-3 revenue grows roughly **160% over the next six months** as traders rotate into real-world asset exposure, metals, indices, oil, and AI-related listings.

#### HIP-4

That is a new revenue vertical not fully captured in the base case. The optionality matters because it expands Hyperliquid from "best perp DEX" toward "on-chain global trading casino with real rails."

## Product And Liquidity Moat

Hyperliquid's edge is not branding. It is market quality.

- Lowest `ADV/OI` ratio among top perp DEXs, suggesting less wash trading and fewer fake points-program volumes.

## Financial Model Summary

The model is intentionally simple.

- Revenue needs to recover from about **$843 million** annualized to about **$1.4 billion** annualized.
- The main driver is continued market-share gain plus HIP-3 growth.
- Tokenholder economics are approximated as:

`P/E = (circulating supply x price) / (annualized revenue x 97% buyback rate)`

Base case:

- Revenue returns to prior peak range
- Buyback mechanism remains intact
- Team selling stays contained
- Market rerates from ~12x to ~25x P/E
- Result: **$150 target**

Stress case:

- Revenue still reaches ~$1.4 billion
- Team selling is much worse than base case
- Multiple stays stuck at ~12x
- Result: roughly **$58**, or still about **+93%** from the March 2026 reference price

## Risks And Mitigants

### 1. Broader crypto bear market

Risk:

- If crypto risk appetite collapses, all exchange-volume-sensitive assets can derate.

Mitigant:

- Exchange-style fee generators have historically held up better than typical altcoins in sideways or weaker tapes.
- `HYPE` has real business activity underneath the token.

### 2. New perp DEX competition

Risk:

- A rival could undercut fees or subsidize volumes.

Mitigant:

- Hyperliquid already appears to lead on real liquidity and slippage, which is harder to fake than token incentives.
- HIP-3 and HIP-4 deepen the moat by widening the venue's product surface.

### 3. Team selling

Risk:

- Token distributions could reaccelerate and weigh on price.

Mitigant:

- Recent selling behavior appears to have collapsed materially.
- Team incentives increasingly align with price and protocol growth.

### 4. Regulatory pressure

Risk:

- On-chain perps are an obvious regulatory target.

Mitigant:

- Permissionless global venues are harder to shut down than centralized operators.
- Regulatory pressure on centralized venues can accelerate migration to decentralized ones.

### 5. Airdrop or future dilution

Risk:

- Unexpected token issuance or incentive programs dilute holders.

Mitigant:

- No major near-term dilution is assumed in the model.
- This should be monitored, but it is not the base-case threat.
